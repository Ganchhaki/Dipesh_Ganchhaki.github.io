02 — Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

37.5%

Fitts's Law Violations

Overlapping belt hitboxes caused frequent mis-grabs that broke flow.

Collapse

Mental Model Collapse

Inconsistent throwable interactions fractured expectations and drove abandonment.

-0.83

Physics Opacity

Immersion dropped due to opaque virtual physics making throws feel unpredictable.

83%

Vestibular Mismatch

High initial Oculomotor strain as the comfort vignette failed during dynamic motion.

03 — Strategic Recommendations

Proposed Data-Driven Solutions

01

Dynamic Spatial Buffering

Increase padding between belt inventory slots by 20% and use "Gaze-Assisted Hitboxes".

02

Trajectory Visualization

Render a diegetic, dashed trajectory arc immediately upon release of a throwable.

03

Standardized Interaction Models

Unify all throwables to a one-handed, gross-motor interaction model (e.g., pre-lit Molotovs) to reduce cognitive load.

04

Omni-Directional Vignetting

Decouple the comfort vignette from strictly forward movement to protect the user's vestibular system.

04 — Limitations & Future Research

Limitations & Future Research

N = 8

Sample Size Constraints

Testing was limited to 8 VR novices. Real‑world physical fatigue over longer sessions was not fully measured.

Next Steps

Prototype A/B Testing

Gaze-Assisted and Omni-directional strategies are formulated theories. Next: mathematically validate error reduction.
