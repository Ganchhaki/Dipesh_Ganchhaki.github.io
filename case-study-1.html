The Project

AimXR: Tactical VR

AimXR is a tactical VR shooter where high-stakes combat demands seamless interaction and deep immersion.

Objective: Optimize interaction flow to enhance player immersion and combat satisfaction.
Focus: Seamless Mechanics, Player Intuition, and Interaction Fidelity.
Target: Creating an effortless "flow state" for players in high-stakes scenarios.
Methodology

Mixed-Methods Evaluation

A controlled 20-minute session alternating between active combat and structured evaluation.

Quantitative: GEQ (Immersion) & VRSQ (Cybersickness) metrics.
Qualitative: Thematic coding of video observations and post-task interviews.
Sample: N = 8 participants (primarily VR novices).
DATA ANALYSIS

What the Data Revealed

37.5%

Targeting Errors

Overlapping belt hitboxes caused frequent mis-grabs breaking interaction flow.

-0.83

Unpredictable Physics

Inability to gauge virtual physics led to significant precision drop in throwable interactions.

High frequency of targeting errors in inventory hitboxes directly correlated with reduced combat efficiency.

A steady decline in immersion scores was observed as cumulative interaction friction broke the user's flow state.

Problem Statement

"How might we improve learnability and gameplay experience by identifying and mitigating interaction friction through usability testing?"

Analysis

Critical Usability Failures

Hitbox Overlap

Spatial inventory slots (belt) were too close, leading to accidental selection of wrong items during combat stress.

Mental Model Collapse

Inconsistent throwable interactions (some lit, some not) fractured user expectations and drove abandonment.

Unpredictable Physics

Virtual physics felt unpredictable; users couldn't gauge throw distance, resulting in a -0.83 score in precision.

Solutions

Data-Driven Strategic Fixes

Spatial Buffering

+20% padding between belt inventory slots to eliminate mis-grabs.

Trajectory Viz

Diegetic dashed arc upon release to provide immediate feedback on physics.

Unified Interaction

Standardizing all throwables to a one-handed motor model to reduce cognitive load.

Limitations

Constraints & Future Research

Sample Size (N=8)

Limited scale prevents broad generalization across all age groups and experience levels.

Physical Fatigue

Longer play sessions (1hr+) need measurement to understand compounding muscle fatigue in tactical stances.

Mathematical Validation

Next phase requires A/B testing the proposed "Gaze-Assisted" hitboxes to quantify error reduction.
