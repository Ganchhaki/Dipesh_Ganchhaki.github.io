The Simulator Hurdle

Why MR and not VR?

Fully virtual simulators demand a complete digital rebuild of every car system — before a single user is onboarded.

Efficiency Mixed Reality uses the real car as the platform — zero duplication needed.
Fragility Every new model year or OTA update breaks the virtual twin entirely.
Flow 01

Spatial Handshake

The system uses LiDAR to anchor the digital experience to the physical cockpit geometry.

Anchoring: Persistent world-locked UI elements.
Precision: Sub-centimeter alignment with dashboard controls.
Flow 02

Exterior Discovery

Flush door handle spatial anchor with cyan outline and floating info dialog.

Voice: "More info" / "Less info" state management.
Flow 03

Interior Setup

Immediate tactile validation of vehicle intelligence.

Sequence: Automatic seat sliding and height profile sync.
Flow 04

Audio System

The first experiential fork: User-led discovery of infotainment value.

Decision: Experience vs. Skip offering user agency.
Tutorials: Integrated video modules for complex feature sets.
Flow 05

Parallel Tech Stack

Unified anchor-highlight pattern across multiple cabin features.

Features: AI Camera, Valet Mode, and Ambient Lighting.
Flow 06

ADAS Vision

Visualizing the "Invisible Shield" — Radar and Camera fields of view projected in the MR visor.

Sensors: Understanding what the car sees.
Confidence: Removing the fear of autonomous braking.
Spatial Ergonomics

Designing for the Eye

UI placement is optimized for cockpit depth and human field of view.

Focal: Content placed at 0.7m to 1.5m to avoid eye strain.
Zone: Critical alerts within the 30° central vision cone.
Design System

The MR Interface

A glassmorphism-based UI language designed for visibility against complex interior backgrounds.

Glass: High-blur backdrops for readability.
Magnetic: UI elements that "snap" to physical buttons.

Implementation

Constraints & Feasibility

Tracking Accuracy

MR spatial anchors can drift slightly — a tooltip meant to highlight a specific button may float off-target, especially in varying lighting conditions inside a car cabin.

Headset Hesitation

First-time EV buyers — already anxious about tech — may resist wearing an unfamiliar headset before they've even sat comfortably in their new car.

Single User Session

MR onboarding is inherently personal. A family buying one car means only one person gets the guided experience at a time.

Hygiene & Sharing

A headset shared across multiple customers at a dealership raises real comfort and sanitation concerns.

Thermal Limits

15-minute session limit to prevent headset overheating in hot climates.
